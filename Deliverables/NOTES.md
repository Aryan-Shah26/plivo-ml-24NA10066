The fitness function balances cosine similarity against a strict naturalness penalty to prevent the embedding from being fooled by degraded audio. It explicitly penalizes near-silence, severe clipping, and unnaturally low energy variance (flatness). The best result achieved was a final similarity score of 0.7400. I initialized the optimization using a blend of the top-performing stock voices to ensure a stable acoustic foundation. To maximize algorithmic efficiency, the search utilized structured perturbation, mutating only the specific tensor rows activated by the phonemes in the evaluation texts. The similarity score eventually plateaued around iteration 147. This plateau likely occurs because the frozen Kokoro model possesses a strictly bounded latent space. Pushing the tensor values further outside the expected distribution yields diminishing returns and rapidly triggers the naturalness penalty. Furthermore, many of the 256 dimensions likely encode rigid structural speech constraints rather than flexible identity features. Consequently, these structural boundaries prevent infinite similarity scaling without ultimately destroying the audio's intelligibility.
